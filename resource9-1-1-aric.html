Atherosclerosis Risk in Communities Study (ARIC)

Conducted in four U.S. communities and sponsored by the NHLBI, the Atherosclerosis Risk in Communities (ARIC) study investigates the causes and clinical outcomes of atherosclerosis, a disease in which plaque builds up inside the arteries and obstructs blood flow.

This prospective epidemiological study also explores variations in cardiovascular risk factors, medical care, and patient race, gender, location, and time. To date, the ARIC study has led to more than 2,000 peer-reviewed publications.
